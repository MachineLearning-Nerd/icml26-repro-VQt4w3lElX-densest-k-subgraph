**Claim 2 (Theorems 3.6/3.7/3.10):** for **λ > 1 non-integral**, every stationary point of (3) is **either** an integral local maximizer **or** a non-integral strict saddle (with explicit positive curvature).

We enumerate **all** stationary points exactly (KKT, Theorem 3.4) over 15 graphs × k∈{3,4,5} at λ=1.5:

- **8,605 stationary points** enumerated; **every one** classifies cleanly:
  - 853 **integral local maximizers** (Theorem 3.7),
  - 7,752 **non-integral strict saddles** (Theorem 3.10),
  - **0** other.
- Minimum ascent curvature at saddles = **0.625 > 0** (the Hessian M=A+λI has an explicit positive-curvature ascent direction on the constraint tangent).

The dichotomy is exactly the paper's "benign landscape": no spurious non-integral local maxima exist.
